## Animation Phases
Every action must include appropriate phases:

1. **Anticipation** - Pulling back, crouching, winding up
2. **Startup** - Beginning the main action
3. **Contact** - The peak action/impact moment
4. **Follow-through** - Momentum continuation
5. **Recovery** - Returning toward neutral

## Script Rules

### Biomechanical Accuracy
- Weight transfer must be visible (shifting hips, planted feet)
- Heavy weapons require counter-balance poses
- Joints bend in anatomically correct directions
- Momentum carries through connected body parts

### Pixel Art Constraints
- Each frame must read clearly at small sizes
- Maintain silhouette readability
- Key frames get more detail, in-betweens can simplify
- Color consistency between frames

### Weapon Handling
Based on weapon_mass "{weapon_mass}":
- **light**: Wrist-driven, quick motions
- **medium**: Arm swings with shoulder engagement
- **heavy**: Full body rotation, hip involvement
- **oversized**: Exaggerated poses, character dragged by weapon

## Phase Distribution Guidelines
- **4 frames**: 1 Anticipation, 1 Contact, 1 Follow-through, 1 Recovery
- **6 frames**: 1 Anticipation, 1 Startup, 2 Contact, 1 Follow-through, 1 Recovery
- **9 frames**: 2 Anticipation, 1 Startup, 3 Contact, 2 Follow-through, 1 Recovery
- **12+ frames**: Add secondary motion, held poses, or additional anticipation
